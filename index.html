High-achieving PhD Candidate in the School of Physics at The University of Melbourne with a strong portfolio of innovation in X-ray science. Successfully developed a novel analytical technique ("Binary Data Splicing") and the XR-HERFD methodology to process complex spectroscopic data. Discovery papers in Nature portfolio and high ranked IUCR journals for finding a new electronic structure (Hidden Satellites and Satellites) in transition metals, contributing significantly to fundamental physics with interesting integration of advanced Principal Component Analysis. Recipient of highly competitive beamtime for seven distinct experiments during 2024, 2025 and 2026 at the Diamond Light Source (UK). Brings advanced computational skills in Python and statistical modeling, seeking to apply rigorous problem-solving abilities in Post-Doctoral research, CSIRO projects, ANSTO Beamlines or quantitative Industrial roles.
